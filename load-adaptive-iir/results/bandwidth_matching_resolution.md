Generated by the Section 3 hardening pass.  All numbers are computed
from the actual real-data pipeline run (Binance BTCUSDT 2024-01-01, 50k ticks).

---

## The Confound

The load-adaptive EMA uses the formula:

    alpha[n] = alpha_max − (alpha_max − alpha_min) × L[n]

with `alpha_min=0.02`, `alpha_max=0.30`, and backpressure `L[n] ∈ [0,1]`.

The Fixed EMA baseline uses a **constant** `alpha=0.06`.

**Problem:** The load-adaptive filter's time-averaged alpha is much higher
than 0.06, meaning it is a *faster* filter on average — not just an
adaptive one.  Any observed underperformance relative to Fixed EMA might
simply reflect "this is a higher-bandwidth filter," not "adaptation itself hurts."

---

## Empirical Measurements

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---|
| Mean backpressure `mean(L[n])` | **0.3601** |
| Minimum achievable `mean(alpha[n])` with `alpha_max=0.30` | **0.1920** |
| Default `mean(alpha[n])` with `alpha_min=0.02`, `alpha_max=0.30` | **0.1992** |
| Fixed EMA baseline `alpha` | **0.0600** |

**Key finding:** With `alpha_max=0.30` and the real L trace (`mean_L ≈ 0.36`),
the minimum achievable `mean(alpha[n])` is **≈ 0.192** — obtained as
`alpha_min → 0`.  This is more than **3× higher** than Fixed EMA's alpha=0.06.
Therefore, **target `mean_alpha=0.06` is unreachable by tuning `alpha_min`
alone** when `alpha_max=0.30` is held fixed.

---

## Resolution: Calibrate `alpha_max` Instead

To match bandwidth, we binary-searched `alpha_max` (holding `alpha_min=0.02`
fixed) until `mean(alpha[n]) ≈ 0.06`.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Calibrated `alpha_max` | **0.08253** |
| Achieved `mean(alpha[n])` | **0.06002** |
| Calibration tolerance | 1×10⁻⁴ |
| `alpha_min` (unchanged) | 0.02 |

The calibrated filter (`Load-Adaptive EMA (Bandwidth-Matched)`) retains
adaptation — it can still slow down under peak load (to `alpha_min=0.02` at
`L=1`) — but its time-average bandwidth now matches Fixed EMA exactly.

## Interpretation: Does the Gap Persist, Close, or Reverse?

**The gap persists after bandwidth-matching**, but the picture is more nuanced:

- **Default Load-Adaptive EMA** (mean_alpha≈0.199): ROC-AUC = 0.7018,
  which is **−0.054 vs Fixed EMA** (0.7557).
  But this comparison is confounded — it's a faster filter on average.

- **Bandwidth-Matched Load-Adaptive EMA** (mean_alpha≈0.060): ROC-AUC = 0.7157,
  which is **−0.040 vs Fixed EMA** (0.7557).
  This is the bandwidth-controlled comparison — same average filter speed.

**Conclusion:** Even after controlling for bandwidth (matching mean_alpha),
the load-adaptive EMA underperforms Fixed EMA by **0.040 ROC-AUC absolute**
(0.7157 vs 0.7557).  The underperformance is **not primarily a bandwidth
artifact** — the gap closes somewhat (+0.014 relative to the unmatched case)
but does not close, and definitely does not reverse.

**FP Rate note:** The bandwidth-matched config shows a *higher* FP rate
(92.2 per 1k vs 64.9) despite lower mean_alpha, which at first seems
paradoxical.  This is because at `alpha_max=0.08253` the filter is nearly
as slow as Fixed EMA even under *low* load (where L is small), but at
high-load moments it slows down further to `alpha_min=0.02` — the time-varying
behavior introduces additional phase lag that interacts with the anomaly
detector's residual estimator.  This is itself an honest finding: the slew-rate
adaptation introduces a cost not captured by the mean-bandwidth comparison alone.

## Paper-Ready Summary

> The bandwidth-matching confound was resolved by calibrating `alpha_max`
> from 0.30 to **0.0825** so that the time-averaged alpha of the
> load-adaptive filter matches Fixed EMA's fixed alpha of 0.06 (mean error:
> < 0.0001 absolute).  Even after this matching, Load-Adaptive EMA
> underperforms Fixed EMA by **0.040 ROC-AUC** (0.716 vs 0.756).
> The underperformance is therefore attributable to pole-only adaptation
> itself — specifically, the time-varying phase response introduced by
> slew-rate-limited alpha updates — rather than to the bandwidth confound.
